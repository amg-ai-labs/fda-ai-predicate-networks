About This Dashboard

This graph visualises predicate relationships among FDA-approved AI/ML-enabled medical devices. The FDA database is periodically updated.

The FDA uses three main regulatory pathways to approve AI-enabled medical devices:

  1. Premarket Approval (PMA) — rigorous review for high-risk devices, requires clinical evidence
  2. De Novo Classification — for first-of-a-kind, low/moderate-risk devices without a predicate
  3. 510(k) Premarket Clearance — the most common pathway, based on substantial equivalence to a previously approved device (the predicate)

Devices can be filtered by specialty, pathway, and subspecialty. Search for a specific device by its FDA submission number to isolate and highlight its predicate network. Click any device in the graph for detailed information including a link to the official clearance summary.

Lines between devices represent predicate relationships — where one device was cleared based on equivalence to another. The colour of each device indicates its predicate provenance: whether the predicate is an AI device within the same specialty (green), an AI device from a different specialty (blue), a non-AI device outside the FDA AI/ML database (terracotta), or whether no predicate exists as in De Novo approvals (gold).

We aim to regularly update these graphs with more specialties, features and up-to-date devices.

Hover over terms below for definitions:

Predicate Device
A previously approved device used to support a new submission's safety and effectiveness through the 510(k) pathway.
Predicate Network
A visual map of how devices are connected by their predicate approvals, showing chains of regulatory equivalence.
Graph Lines
Vertical links between a device and its predicate. Devices sharing a common ancestor form visible tree structures.
Vertical Axis
Shows approval date — oldest devices at the bottom, most recent at the top.
Horizontal Axis
Devices are grouped by predicate tree. Larger, more connected trees appear towards the centre.
Submission Number
A unique FDA identifier for each device application (e.g. K231335, DEN170073).
Product Code
FDA-assigned classification code indicating the device's intended use and technology type.
Predicate Provenance
Classification of where a device's predicate sits: within the same specialty's AI devices, in another specialty, outside the AI/ML database entirely, or absent (De Novo).
